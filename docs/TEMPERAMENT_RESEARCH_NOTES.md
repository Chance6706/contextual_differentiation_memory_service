# Temperament Plan — Research Notes & Citations

Companion to [`TEMPERAMENT_PLAN.md`](TEMPERAMENT_PLAN.md). Sources gathered for the
represented-side grounding (the principle that *a facsimile is by nature representative*).
Grouped by theme. **Confidence tiers** and **genuine scholarly conflicts** are flagged because a
faithful facsimile must honor the disputes, not just one camp (CLAUDE.md §8). Many figures were
corroborated via SEP/encyclopedic summaries of paywalled primaries; exact page-pinned quotations

## Personality stability & change (the rate/direction/bound anchors)
- Roberts & DelVecchio 2000, *Psych Bulletin* 126:3–25 — rank-order stability rises .31→.74, never 1.0. https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/10668348/
- Roberts, Walton & Viechtbauer 2006, *Psych Bulletin* 132:1–25 — maturity principle, mean-level change continues past 30. https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/16435954/
- Roberts & Mroczek 2008, *Curr Dir Psych Sci* 17:31–35 — ~0.1 SD/decade; ~0.5–1.0 SD lifetime. https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC2743415/
- Srivastava, John, Gosling & Potter 2003, *JPSP* 84:1041 — "set like plaster or persistent change?" change continues. APA PDF: https://www.apa.org/pubs/journals/releases/psp-8451041.pdf
- **CONFLICT:** Terracciano, Costa & McCrae 2006 (PMC2680603) "plasticity after 30" defends stability — rank-order vs mean-level metric confusion. Consensus: real but gradual lifelong change.
- Mechanisms: Roberts, Wood & Caspi 2008 (Handbook of Personality, 3rd ed.); corresponsive/cumulative-continuity — Roberts, Caspi & Moffitt 2003, *JPSP* 84:582 (Dunedin). https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pmc/articles/PMC3722306/
- Volitional change: Hudson & Fraley 2015, *JPSP* 109:490 (https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/25822032/); Stieger et al. 2021 PNAS PEACH app (https://www.pnas.org/doi/10.1073/pnas.2017548118); Roberts, Luo et al. 2017 intervention meta-analysis (207 studies, neuroticism d≈−0.4).
- Life-event effects CONTESTED: Bühler et al. 2024 (small, work>love); set points shiftable — see below.

## Temperament vs character (the substrate/developable gradient)
- Cloninger, Svrakic & Przybeck 1993, *Arch Gen Psychiatry* 50:975 — TCI: temperament (NS/HA/RD/Persistence) vs character (SD/Coop/ST). https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/8250684/
- **CONFLICT:** Gillespie et al. 2003 (PMC4448136) — character ≈ as heritable as temperament (27–44% vs 30–41%), genetically independent → use a plasticity *gradient*, not a frozen/developable wall. TCI overlaps Big Five (De Fruyt 2000).
- Rothbart (reactivity + effortful control); Thomas & Chess NYLS (9 dims, goodness-of-fit); Kagan (high/low reactivity, amygdala, only *partially* stable). https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Temperament

## Dynamics / math (the OU/STARTS/Fleeson formal core)
- Kenny & Zautra 1995 STARTS (stable trait + AR drift + state/error), *J Consult Clin Psych* 63:52. https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/7896990/
- Steyer et al. 1999 Latent State-Trait theory, *Eur J Pers* 13:389.
- Fleeson 2001 density-distributions-of-states, *JPSP* 80:1011 — trait = mean of a state distribution. https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Whole_Trait_Theory
- **PersDyn / BHOUM (the OU precedent):** Sosnowska, De Fruyt & Hofmans 2019, *Front Psychol* 10:2264 — fits OU `dΘ=β(μ−Θ)dt+ξ` to personality states; β scale is arbitrary/timescale-dependent. https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC6805692/
- Ornstein–Uhlenbeck process (math). https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ornstein%E2%80%93Uhlenbeck_process
- **HUMILITY:** Kording "Attractors are usually not mechanisms" (https://kording.substack.com/p/attractors-are-usually-not-mechanisms); Eronen & Bringmann 2021 theory crisis (https://journals.sagepub.com/doi/10.1177/1745691620970586); Gelfand et al. 2018 (PMC6189449) — "attractor/orbit" is descriptive metaphor, not validated mechanism.

## Set-point / well-being (bounds are real but shiftable → Growth exception)
- Brickman & Campbell 1971 hedonic treadmill; Diener, Lucas & Scollon 2006 "five revisions" (consensus: revise not discard). https://labs.psychology.illinois.edu/~ediener/Documents/Diener-Lucas-Scollon_2006.pdf
- Set points DO shift: Lucas et al. 2004 (unemployment, Psych Sci 15:8); Lucas 2007 (disability, JPSP 92:717). Heritable ~50%: Lykken & Tellegen 1996. **CONFLICT:** Headey 2010 "needs replacing" (minority view) vs Diener/Lucas "revise."

## Identity pathology (the degeneracy ↔ guard mapping)
- Marcia 1966, *JPSP* 3:551 — diffusion/foreclosure/moratorium/achievement (exploration × commitment); foreclosure = commitment w/o exploration (authoritarian, p<.001). Kroger, Martinussen & Marcia 2010 (124 studies; progressive .36/regressive .15/stable .49). https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/20004962/
- BPD: DSM-5 Crit 3 "markedly and persistently unstable self-image"; Wilkinson-Ryan & Westen 2000, *Am J Psychiatry* 157:528 — 4 factors (role absorption, painful incoherence [most diagnostic], inconsistency, lack of commitment). Linehan biosocial (sensitivity + slow return). https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/10739411/
- Rumination: Nolen-Hoeksema 1991/2008 RST; Bower 1981 mood-congruent memory; Beck cognitive triad. Critical slowing down: van de Leemput et al. 2014 PNAS 111:87 — BUT replication weak ~33% (Smit et al. 2025; Helmich et al. 2024 critique, Nat Rev Psychol). https://www.pnas.org/doi/10.1073/pnas.1312114110
- Confirmation bias/echo chambers: Nickerson 1998; Wason 1960; Nguyen 2020 *Episteme* (bubble vs chamber); info cascades Bikhchandani, Hirshleifer & Welch 1992; correlated-evidence double-counting Bovens & Hartmann 2003.
- Dissonance/self-perception (THE self-referential pump): Festinger 1957; Festinger & Carlsmith 1959 ($1>$20); Bem 1967/1972 (infer self from own behavior); Fazio, Zanna & Cooper 1977 (latitude of acceptance/rejection — needs external arousal signal); foot-in-the-door Freedman & Fraser 1966; over-justification Lepper, Greene & Nisbett 1973; Swann self-verification; Merton self-fulfilling prophecy.
- Helplessness/protective: Maier & Seligman 2016 *Psych Review* 123:349 — passivity is the DEFAULT, control is learned (https://ppc.sas.upenn.edu/sites/default/files/learnedhelplessnessat50.pdf); kindling Post 1992; inhibitory learning needs PREDICTION ERROR — Craske et al. 2014 *Behav Res Ther* 58:10; self-complexity Linville 1985/87 (buffering empirically WEAK — Rafaeli-Mor & Steinberg 2002).
- Reconsolidation (why re-reading drifts — but gated by prediction error): Nader, Schafe & LeDoux 2000 Nature 406:722; Sevenster, Beckers & Kindt 2013 Science 339:830 (PE necessary); Schiller et al. 2010 Nature 463:49. **Heavily caveated:** Elsey, Van Ast & Kindt 2018 (Psych Bulletin review); flagship results failed to replicate; overwrite-vs-interference unresolved.

## Philosophy of personal identity (the persistence-invariant grounding)
- Parfit, *Reasons and Persons* 1984, §§78–79, 87–89: connectedness (direct, degreed, non-transitive) vs continuity (overlapping chains of strong connectedness, transitive); Relation R = what matters, not numerical identity; identity a matter of degree / "empty questions" (combined spectrum). SEP: https://plato.stanford.edu/entries/identity-personal/ ; https://plato.stanford.edu/entries/identity-ethics/
- Parfit's reductionism is NOT eliminativism (persons exist, just not as "separately existing entities"); §95 "liberating and consoling" / glass-tunnel (p.281); Appendix J aligns with Buddhist no-self.
- Locke (Essay 2.27, consciousness criterion — NOT strictly "memory"; Atherton/Winkler); Reid brave-officer (transitivity failure); Ship of Theseus (SEP Identity Over Time — form vs matter).
- Narrative identity: Ricoeur idem/ipse; MacIntyre story-telling animal; McAdams & McLean 2013 (agency/communion, redemption/contamination, coherence↔well-being); **DISSENT:** Strawson 2004 "Against Narrativity."
- Skeptical stress-test: Hume bundle theory (Treatise 1.4.6; recanted in Appendix); Buddhist anatta (two truths: conventionally real, ultimately empty); **Korsgaard** "Unity of Agency" 1989 / *Self-Constitution* 2009 — self is practically *constituted* by agency (contested by Enoch's "shmagency"). → temperament = useful control-fiction, held operator-only.
